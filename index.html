    Online Fake News Detection Using NLP & Computer Vision

    This project explores how artificial intelligence—specifically NLP and computer vision—can be used to automatically detect and flag fake news across digital platforms. By analyzing both textual patterns and visual clues, the system aims to provide a robust approach to identifying misleading content.

    Deepfake Video Detection

    Deepfake videos pose a serious threat by fabricating realistic audio-visual content. This module focuses on detecting manipulated video using AI-based temporal and spatial inconsistencies, helping prevent the spread of malicious or deceptive media.

    Image Manipulation & AI Image Detection

    Image-based fake news often involves subtle edits or fully AI-generated visuals. Through techniques like computer vision and artifact detection, this component targets tampered and synthetic images to ensure content integrity.

    Fake News Identification in English Language and Generated Voice Identification

    This section tackles both textual and auditory misinformation. It uses NLP techniques to scrutinize English-language articles for fake news while applying deep learning methods to detect AI-generated voices in digital media.

    Sinhala Fake News Identification and Identifying Political Bias

    This part of the research addresses fake news in the Sinhala language, focusing on misinformation related to local events and politics. It also investigates how political bias can influence news narratives, using sentiment and semantic analysis.

Real-time data processing and cloud integration are pivotal for the system's functionality. Casado-Vara et al.'s research [5] discusses cloud computing in the context of real-time data processing, providing valuable insights into system architecture. The mobile application's development for the Android platform finds support in research by Mishra et al. [6], who detail best practices in mobile app development and usability considerations.

    Research Gap

    Lack of Integrated Misinformation Detection

    While numerous studies address fake news, deepfakes, or manipulated images independently, very few systems integrate all these modalities into one unified and explainable platform for cross-verification.

    Limited Focus on Multilingual Content

    Existing research predominantly focuses on English language misinformation. There is a significant gap in systems that can analyze content in low-resource languages like Sinhala for fake news or political bias.

    Explainability in Deepfake Detection

    Most deepfake detection models are black-box solutions. There is limited research on integrating explainable AI (XAI) to help users understand why a piece of content is classified as fake or real.

    Lack of User-Friendly Verification Tools

    Many state-of-the-art solutions are academic or server-based models with no accessible interface. There is a need for practical, real-time, user-friendly platforms for non-technical users to verify multimedia content.

    Research Problems

    Multimodal Fake Content Detection

    How can a unified system be developed to effectively detect and differentiate between various types of fake content, including deepfake videos, AI-generated images, and text-based misinformation?

    Multilingual Fake News Analysis

    What methods can be employed to improve fake news detection in both high-resource (English) and low-resource (Sinhala) languages while maintaining high accuracy and cultural relevance?

    Explainable AI for Misinformation

    How can explainable AI techniques be integrated into misinformation detection systems to provide users with interpretable reasons for content being flagged as fake or biased?

    Real-Time and Scalable Verification

    What strategies can be used to enable real-time content verification across text, images, and videos while ensuring the system remains scalable and efficient for public use?

    Main Objectives

    The main objective was to develop an intelligent, user-friendly web platform that detects and explains various forms of digital misinformation—including deepfake videos, AI-manipulated images, fake news in English and Sinhala, and political bias in Sinhala media—using advanced machine learning and explainable AI techniques. The goal is to help users verify content more easily, promote digital literacy, and reduce the spread of misleading information across online platforms.

    English Fake News & Voice Analysis

    Detect misleading English news articles and analyze spoken content to identify signs of misinformation.

    Sinhala Fake News & Bias Detection

    Identify fake news and political bias in Sinhala media using linguistic analysis and user feedback.

    Image Analysis System

    Distinguish between genuine, AI-generated, and manipulated images using forensic and deep learning tools.

    Deepfake Video Detection System

    Analyze and classify videos to detect deepfake manipulations using explainable AI techniques.

    Sub Objectives

    Multimodal Content Verification

    Develop robust AI models to detect fake content across multiple formats: videos (deepfakes), images, and text (including Sinhala and English).

    Multilingual Fake News Detection

    Enable accurate identification of fake news articles in multiple languages, including low-resource languages like Sinhala.

    Explainable AI Integration

    Integrate explainable AI techniques (e.g., CAM, attention maps) to help users understand why a piece of content was flagged as fake.

    Real-Time Detection and Reporting

    Provide users with near real-time analysis of uploaded content and generate downloadable verification reports for transparency.

    Bias and Propaganda Analysis

    Analyze news sources for political or ideological bias to detect potential propaganda, especially in regional contexts.
